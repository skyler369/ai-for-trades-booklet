2. HOW TO FILL IN THE BRACKETS

The Bracket System Explained

Every prompt uses [brackets like this]. This is where YOU add your information.

Example:

Write a quote for [Customer Name]

You change it to:

Write a quote for John Smith

Visual Example

Prompt:

Write a professional quote for a plumbing job.

Customer: [Customer Name]
Service: [What you're doing]

What you type:

Write a professional quote for a plumbing job.

Customer: John Smith
Service: Fix leaky faucet under kitchen sink

Result: A complete, professional quote tailored to John Smith’s job.

Pro Tip

Don’t remove the brackets entirely — replace the inside text only. The brackets help you see where information goes.

📞 PROMPT 4: Explain a Technical Problem to a Customer

Copy This:

Explain [technical problem, e.g., what a cracked heat exchanger is, why a backed-up sewer line is dangerous, what polybutylene piping failure looks like] in simple terms that a homeowner with no technical knowledge would understand.

Use:
- Simple words (no jargon)
- 1-2 short paragraphs
- A real-life analogy
- Why it matters (safety/cost/health hazard)

🛡️ PROMPT 11: Customer Objection Handler

Copy This:

Write a professional response to a customer who says "[objection, e.g., That's too expensive, I need to get another quote, Can you do it cheaper]"

Context:
- Service: [What they're getting]
- Original quote: $[amount]
- Customer concern: [Their specific objection]

Write a response that:
- Acknowledges their concern
- Explains the value/why the price is fair
- Offers a solution or alternative
- Keeps the door open for them to say yes

Tone: Understanding, not defensive. Help them feel confident in their decision.

4. CUSTOMER OBJECTION HANDLER

Common Objections & How to Respond

“That’s too expensive.” - Acknowledge: “I understand budget is a concern.” - Value: “This price includes [list what’s included].” - Option: “We can also [offer a simpler alternative].”

“I need to get another quote.” - Acknowledge: “Absolutely, getting multiple quotes is smart.” - Value: “Here’s what makes us different [unique selling points].” - Follow-up: “Let me know if you have questions after the other quotes.”

“Can you do it cheaper?” - Acknowledge: “I want to make this work for you.” - Explain: “Here’s what goes into the price [labor, parts, warranty].” - Option: “We could [suggest alternatives].”

“I want to think about it.” - Acknowledge: “Of course, this is a big decision.” - Urgency: “Just so you know, we have [availability/time sensitivity].” - Follow-up: “When would be a good time to check in?”

5. SEASONAL & SAFETY CHECKLISTS

Seasonal Maintenance Checklist Prompts

Spring (Post-Winter)

Create a spring plumbing maintenance checklist for a homeowner. Include:
- Check for leaky faucets and pipes
- Test water heater pressure relief valve
- Clean out gutters and downspouts
- Inspect outdoor faucets and sprinkler systems
- Check for signs of water damage
- Water heater flushing

Fall (Pre-Winter)

Create a fall plumbing maintenance checklist for a homeowner. Include:
- Insulate exposed pipes
- Disconnect outdoor hoses
- Service water heater
- Check furnace/boiler
- Seal air leaks
- Sump pump test
- Emergency prep kit list

Safety Checklist Prompt

Create a job site safety checklist for a plumbing job. Include:
- Personal protective equipment (PPE)
- Tool inspection
- Area safety (ventilation, access)
- Electrical hazards to watch for
- Gas line safety protocols
- Customer notification procedures
- Cleanup and disposal
- Final inspection points

7. TROUBLESHOOTING

“The AI gave me something weird”

Fix: Try being more specific. Add details like: - “Make it shorter” - “Use simpler words” - “Make it more professional” - “Add more detail”

“I’m not getting what I want”

Fix: Try this trick:

Ignore previous instructions. Now do [X].

“This is too complicated”

Fix: Start with the shortest prompt in this booklet. You don’t have to use them all — just the ones you need.

“The AI doesn’t know my area”

Fix: Add your region at the end of any prompt: - “Ontario” (or your local province/state) - “Alberta” - “British Columbia” - Or simply your city and region

“I need something faster”

Fix: Use the Quick Reference Card above. Pick the prompt that matches your need, copy-paste, fill in brackets, done.
